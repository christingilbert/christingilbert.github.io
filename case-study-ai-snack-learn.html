The Situation

Traditional campaign photoshoots for digital campaigns typically cost ~$7,000 per campaign, including photography, post-production, coordination, and asset preparation.

As campaign timelines accelerated and the need for more digital assets increased, the team needed a way to produce high-quality visuals faster while maintaining brand consistency and reducing production costs.

The Challenge

The creative team came primarily from print, packaging, and traditional graphic design backgrounds. While highly skilled visually, most had little exposure to generative AI tools or digital content production workflows.

As AI tools began appearing across the industry, there was curiosity within the team but no shared understanding of how these tools could fit into the existing creative process. Designers were already exploring AI individually. What the team needed was a structured workflow that could turn those experiments into production-ready digital assets.

For AI to become a practical part of the creative workflow, the team needed a clear structure for prompts, asset generation, and brand alignment.

At the same time, different stakeholders had different needs.

  • Designers needed practical guidance on how to generate assets that could support digital campaigns.
  • The Creative Director needed content guardrails to ensure AI-generated visuals stayed aligned with brand and regulatory standards.
  • Senior leadership needed enough understanding of the process to feel confident supporting the adoption of these tools.

The goal was not simply introducing AI. It was building a framework that connected experimentation, brand guidelines, and executive confidence.

The Approach

To address this, I designed and delivered a three-session internal learning series called Snack & Learn: AI for Image and Video Generation.

The sessions were structured to gradually move the team from curiosity to capability: introducing not just tools, but the full workflow required to generate usable campaign assets.

01
Text to Image
Prompt structure fundamentals, tool stack orientation across ChatGPT, Midjourney, Magnific AI, and Adobe Firefly. Live brief: product in a studio setting.
02
Still to Motion
Text-to-video and image-to-video workflows, AI model vs. platform distinctions, and structured JSON-style prompt architecture. Live brief: human element in a spa setting.
03
Content to Campaign
Campaign-level AI workflow, master prompt development, content guardrails, prompt ownership governance, and derivative asset system design across channels.
The goal was to show how generative AI could expand what was possible with existing assets, reshaping how the team approaches creative production.

The sessions were designed to support two audiences simultaneously: the designers and producers executing the work, and the executives who needed enough fluency to govern it. Technical depth without inaccessibility.

AI Production Workflow
Governance & Brand Alignment

Alongside the training, I introduced content guardrails and prompt ownership practices. These guidelines ensured that AI-generated visuals remained aligned with brand standards while also providing executives with clarity about where AI could, and could not, be used.

AI is approved for
  • Backgrounds & environments
  • Props and secondary elements
  • Mood and lighting exploration
  • Concept and moodboard generation
  • Campaign visual testing
AI is not approved for
  • Product efficacy claims
  • Skin transformations or results
  • Product textures
  • Before/after visuals
  • Realistic human results tied to products

Tool access guardrails covered approved shared accounts, prompt logging requirements, and prompt ownership protocol for assets entering paid media: creating clear visibility from brief to final asset.

Business Impact
Implemented an AI-enabled content production workflow that reduced campaign visual production costs by ~70% and accelerated asset production 3–4× compared to traditional photoshoots.

Campaign visuals were produced internally by combining existing product assets with AI-generated environments, significantly reducing the need for traditional full-scale photoshoots.

  • Estimated savings per campaign: ~$4–5K compared to traditional photoshoot production
  • Estimated annual impact: ~60–70% reduction in visual production costs across campaigns
  • Campaign production cycles reduced from ~6 weeks to 3–4 weeks, a 33–50% reduction in time-to-delivery
  • External shoot dependency reduced by ~30%, shifting production capacity toward internal AI-assisted workflows

Additional outcomes included reduced production coordination and scheduling overhead, increased internal capability with generative AI tools, and scalable reuse of existing product assets.

The Outcomes

By the end of the series, the team had produced a complete Evergreen 2026 AI visual system: a master prompt, approved variant prompts, and a derivative asset architecture mapped to website, social, email, and paid media. Each campaign now yields 15–20 on-brand still images and 2 motion assets for the website carousel, all generated within a documented, repeatable workflow.

  • A shared prompt framework enabling brand-aligned generation without per-asset creative direction
  • A prompt library converting individual experiments into durable institutional knowledge
  • A governance document giving executives confidence in AI's role and boundaries within the brand
