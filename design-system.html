Plum & Silk

The visual language for christingilbert.github.io. A warm near-white silk ground, deep plum for weight, and a mauve accent tuned in two steps: mauve on light, rose on dark. Every value here is verified to WCAG AA. Copy the tokens into any page to keep the site consistent.

v2: Plum & Silk palette (plum ink, almond-silk grounds, mauve accent shifting to rose on dark). v1.1: single-color headings and italic quotes. v1: initial Ink & Ochre system.

Color tokens

Two warm light tones carry the page, plum supplies the dark weight, and one mauve is the only chromatic accent. Separation comes from color blocking, not lines.

Why two mauves? A single mid-tone mauve can't pass contrast on both a near-white ground and the plum band: too light on silk, too dark on plum. So the accent deepens to #6E4658 on light and lifts to a rose #D98C95 on dark. Same family, two steps. Never use rose on light or mauve on dark.

Copy-paste tokens

Drop this :root at the top of any page's stylesheet. The last two were hardcoded before; promote them to variables so dark sections are reusable.

:root {
  /* neutrals */
  --bg:        #FAF3EF;  /* silk — ground; light text on dark */
  --surface:   #EACDC2;  /* almond — cards & alt sections */
  --dark:      #1A1423;  /* plum — feature band, footer */
  --ink-card:  #372549;  /* violet — cards on the dark band */
  /* text (all collapse to plum on light) */
  --headline:  #1A1423;
  --body:      #1A1423;
  --muted:     #1A1423;
  /* accent — one mauve, two steps */
  --accent:    #6E4658;  /* mauve, on LIGHT surfaces */
  --accent-h:  #523544;  /* hover on light */
  --rose:      #D98C95;  /* on DARK surfaces */
  /* type */
  --serif:     'Playfair Display', Georgia, serif;
  --body-fnt:  'Hanken Grotesk', system-ui, sans-serif;
}

Color blocking

The page separates by fill, never by rules or dividers. Sections alternate silk and almond; one plum band anchors the middle; the footer closes on plum. Grouped items become filled cards with gaps (radius 10–12px, gap ~0.6rem). No borders.

Card fill rule: a card uses the tone its section doesn't. On a silk section, cards are almond; on an almond section, cards are silk; on the plum band, cards are violet. That one-step difference is what makes the blocking read.

Accessibility audit

Headings are a single tone (plum on light, silk on dark), well above AA. Accent emphasis (links, highlighted phrases) clears the 3:1 large-text bar at 7.1:1, so it stays safe even at the smallest sizes. Text over the hero photo (skill tags) depends on the image and can't be guaranteed by static contrast: the warm plum gradient at the photo's base keeps the lower pills legible, so keep important hero text out of bright photo regions.

Reusing this on other pages

A short checklist to bring the rest of the site onto this palette (the Ink & Ochre to Plum & Silk migration).

  • Paste the :root token block into each page's stylesheet, replacing the old Ink & Ochre variables.
  • Find-and-replace the legacy hexes: #211F1C→#1A1423, #8A5015→#6E4658, #743F11→#523544, #F2EEE5→#FAF3EF, #E5DFD2→#EACDC2, #2E2B27→#372549, #D9A24E→#D98C95.
  • Watch for the old colors in rgba() form: overlays use rgba(33,31,28,…) and ochre tints use rgba(138,80,21,…), which hex find-and-replace misses. Convert to rgba(26,20,35,…) and rgba(110,70,88,…).
  • Keep line separators out: color-block instead. Alternate silk/almond, give grouped items a contrasting fill, radius 10–12px, gap 0.6rem.
  • Use mauve --accent on light surfaces only; switch to --rose for any text on the plum band or footer.
  • Keep headings a single color: plum on light, silk on dark. No accent inside a heading; reserve it for eyebrows, links and highlighted phrases.
  • Pair faces by role: Playfair for display, Hanken for everything else. Italics are reserved for quotes, pull-quotes and the occasional personal aside.
  • On any new dark section, set text to --bg (silk), cards to --ink-card (violet), accents to --rose, and re-check contrast.
