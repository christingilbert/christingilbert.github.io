Ink & Ochre

The visual language for christingilbert.github.io. A warm paper ground, near-black ink for weight, and a single ochre accent tuned in two steps for light and dark. Every value here is verified to WCAG AA. Copy the tokens into any page to keep the site consistent.

Color tokens

Two warm neutrals carry the page, ink supplies the dark weight, and one ochre is the only chromatic accent. Separation comes from color blocking, not lines.

Why two ochres? A single mid-tone orange can't pass contrast on both a near-white ground and near-black ink — too light on paper, too dark on ink. So the accent deepens to #8A5015 on light and lightens to #D9A24E on dark. Same hue, two steps. Never use gold on light or ochre on dark.

Copy-paste tokens

Drop this :root at the top of any page's stylesheet. The last two were hardcoded before — promote them to variables so dark sections are reusable.

:root {
  /* neutrals */
  --bg:        #F2EEE5;  /* paper — ground; text on dark */
  --surface:   #E5DFD2;  /* block — cards & alt sections */
  --dark:      #211F1C;  /* ink — feature band, footer */
  --ink-card:  #2E2B27;  /* cards on the ink band */
  /* text (all collapse to ink on light) */
  --headline:  #211F1C;
  --body:      #211F1C;
  --muted:     #211F1C;
  /* accent — one ochre, two steps */
  --accent:    #8A5015;  /* on LIGHT surfaces */
  --accent-h:  #743F11;  /* hover on light */
  --gold:      #D9A24E;  /* on DARK surfaces */
  /* type */
  --serif:     'Playfair Display', Georgia, serif;
  --body-fnt:  'Hanken Grotesk', system-ui, sans-serif;
}

Color blocking

The page separates by fill, never by rules or dividers. Sections alternate paper and block; one ink band anchors the middle; the footer closes on ink. Grouped items become filled cards with gaps (radius 10–12px, gap ~0.6rem) — no borders.

Hero  ·  paper
About  ·  block
Expertise  ·  paper  (cards = block)
In their words  ·  ink band  (cards = ink-card, accent = gold)
Field notes  ·  paper
Footer  ·  ink

Card fill rule: a card uses the neutral its section doesn't. On a paper section, cards are block; on a block section, cards are paper; on the ink band, cards are ink-card. That one-step difference is what makes the blocking read.

Large display ochre (e.g. "Christin", section headings) also clears the 3:1 large-text bar at 5.6:1, so headings are safe even at the deepest pairing. Text over the hero photo (skill tags) depends on the image and can't be guaranteed by static contrast — the warm ink gradient at the photo's base keeps the lower pills legible; keep important hero text out of bright photo regions.

Reusing this on other pages

A short checklist to bring the 14 other pages onto this system.

  • Paste the :root token block into each page's stylesheet, replacing the old navy/terracotta variables.
  • Find-and-replace the legacy hexes: #1C2B3A→#211F1C, #9B4726→#8A5015, #E8E2D9→#F2EEE5, #F0EBE3→#E5DFD2.
  • Watch for navy in rgba() form: gradients and overlays use rgba(28,43,58,…), which hex find-and-replace misses. Convert to rgba(33,31,28,…) or they stay blue.
  • Replace line separators (section border-top, card dividers) with color blocking: alternate paper/block, give grouped items a contrasting fill, radius 10–12px, gap 0.6rem.
  • Use ochre --accent on light surfaces only; switch to --gold for any text on the ink band or footer.
  • Pair faces by role: Playfair for display, Hanken for everything else. No italics except the one deliberate personal aside.
  • On any new dark section, set text to --bg (paper), cards to --ink-card, accents to --gold, and re-check contrast.
