Poem

Cradling Water

Poetry Place Belonging
Something luminous, peripheral and a ghost of cabin its windows holding only water come anyway if you find it let it remain as it is kneel, if you need to cup water in your hands or stay the ducks don't ask who you are the wildflowers don't ask why you came the untrimmed grass will cradle whatever weight you bring or holding nothing of you there is nothing here to prove drink until light moves across the water and you remain without it

I wrote this after standing at the edge of a pond I had no particular reason to visit. Something in the stillness asked nothing of me, and that felt like enough to stay for.

The places that ask nothing of us may be where we most belong.
