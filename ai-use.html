AI Use & Attribution

Philosophy

AI is one of many tools in my creative practice. Rather than relying on a single model or platform, I choose different tools based on the problem I'm trying to solve. As AI becomes part of everyday creative work, I believe transparency is important. This page explains how I use AI and the principles that guide those decisions.

How AI was used on this website

This website was conceived, designed, written and art directed by me. Claude Code assisted with front-end implementation, code refinement and iterative problem solving. All research, information architecture, photography, visual direction and final editorial decisions are my own.

Used AI for
  • Front-end implementation
  • HTML/CSS refinement
  • Rapid prototyping
  • Accessibility checks
  • Exploring alternative solutions
Human-led
  • Research and interviews
  • Information architecture
  • UX and interaction decisions
  • Visual direction
  • Photography
  • Writing and creative judgment
  • Designing AI workflows
  • Choosing the right creative tools

Guiding principles

Creating visually beautiful and accessible experiences comes naturally to me. AI helps reduce repetitive work, speed up implementation and create more space to explore ideas. It allows me to spend more time exploring, iterating and improving the quality of the final work.

I believe AI can accelerate execution, but it should never replace curiosity, empathy, judgment or responsibility.
