The Situation

As digital marketing expanded across multiple channels — landing pages, email, social media, paid advertising, and blog content — the volume of creative assets required for each campaign grew rapidly. While brand guidelines defined the overall identity of the brand, translating those guidelines into consistent digital execution across campaigns required a more structured approach.

Creative teams needed clear systems that could guide campaign messaging, visual styling, photography direction, layout and typography, accessibility standards, and asset specifications for multiple platforms. Without a structured framework, campaigns risked inconsistency and inefficiencies during production.

The Challenge

The main challenge was bridging the gap between brand strategy and production execution. Marketing teams needed campaign concepts, while creative teams needed precise guidance for producing hundreds of assets across platforms.

To support scalable production, the system needed to translate brand guidelines into practical digital rules while maintaining visual consistency across campaigns.

That meant integrating accessibility standards and providing clear direction for photo and video production — not as separate considerations, but as built-in parts of the production process.

The Approach

To address this, I designed a multi-layer creative production system that connects brand identity to campaign execution. The framework includes four interconnected layers, each building on the previous one to ensure campaigns move smoothly from strategy to execution.

01
Digital Visual System
The foundational rules for digital design — typography, color hierarchy, layout structures, and accessibility standards that apply across all digital campaigns.
02
Campaign Creative Guidelines
Translates the visual system into a specific campaign identity, defining messaging, color palette variations, typography usage, layout structures, and asset formats per launch.
03
Photo & Video Creative Concepts
Creative concepts that define narrative, mood, prop styling, wardrobe, lighting, and composition for photo and video production aligned to each campaign.
04
Digital Asset Production
With the framework established, teams produce assets across landing pages, social media, email, blog content, paid advertising, and campaign collection pages.
Layer 01 — Visual System

The visual system establishes the foundational rules for digital design, ensuring assets remain consistent across platforms such as landing pages, social media, email campaigns, and advertising.

  • Typography Defined font hierarchy using Big Caslon CC for headings and Soleil for body text, with structured typography scales and standardized spacing and alignment rules.
  • Color Color palette and contrast guidelines aligned with WCAG accessibility standards, applied consistently across all digital contexts.
  • Accessibility Contrast standards, text overlay requirements, and mobile-safe placement rules embedded into the visual system rather than applied as a final review step.
  • Layout Standardized layout structures for landing pages, social media, email campaigns, and advertising, with defined breakpoints and responsive behavior.
Layer 02 — Campaign Guidelines

Once the foundational system is in place, each campaign receives its own creative guideline layer. This translates the visual system into a specific campaign identity before asset production begins.

For the Spring 2026 launch, the campaign was centered on post-treatment recovery and professional results, emphasizing the relationship between spa treatments and at-home skincare routines. Campaign guidelines defined the messaging, color palette variations, typography usage for launch graphics, digital layout structures, and asset formats — ensuring marketing teams and designers shared a clear creative direction.

Layer 03 — Photo & Video

Campaigns also require consistent visual storytelling. To guide photo and video production, I developed creative concepts that define the narrative, mood, and visual styling for shoots.

For Spring 2026, the concept "Transitional Aftercare" was designed to visually connect spa treatments with at-home skincare routines.

The concept outlined narrative direction for imagery, prop styling and product placement, wardrobe and makeup styling, and lighting conditions and composition. Imagery transitions from professional spa environments to minimalist home settings, visually reinforcing the idea that skincare results continue beyond the spa. This approach ensures campaign photography and video assets remain aligned with both brand identity and marketing messaging.

Outcomes
The system transformed campaign production from a series of isolated design tasks into a repeatable creative framework.
  • Consistent visual identity maintained across campaigns and digital channels
  • Accelerated asset production through standardized formats and shared production rules
  • Marketing, design, and content teams aligned around shared creative guidelines before production begins
  • Accessibility standards integrated into everyday production workflows rather than applied retrospectively
  • A scalable framework that adapts to new campaigns without rebuilding from scratch

Design systems are often associated with product interfaces, but marketing teams benefit equally from structured creative systems. By translating brand identity into practical production frameworks, this system allowed digital campaigns to scale while preserving consistency, accessibility, and storytelling quality.
