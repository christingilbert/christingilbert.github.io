Kumano Kodo, Japan

The first day out of Takijiri-oji rose almost seven hundred metres straight up. Twenty-six pounds on my shoulders. The weight felt literal, maybe it wasn't.

A mayday call cracked through Channel 16. The great blue heron was already long gone — a loner, a master of waiting. A quiet sail had given way to scribbled coordinates.

Round Bowen Race, BC

Some mornings I step onto a racing boat the same way I once stepped into my black dress. There is a space here where femininity does not have to shrink to fit.

Nevada

What do we carve into when life itself is fleeting? Run your palm against fire-coloured stone and feel the story pulsing beneath. The petroglyphs returned me to a child's morning in East Java.

Mount Shuksan, Washington

The mountain yields to pressure in order to rise. The glacier resists just enough to remain coherent. What can feel like nothing happening may simply be change moving more slowly than we expect.
